🎓 THE THREE TASKS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TASK 1: EMAIL CLASSIFICATION (Easy)
────────────────────────────────────
Input:    EmailData(subject, body, sender)
Output:   EmailAction(category: "admission"|"academic"|"behavioral"|"health"|"extracurricular"|"other")
Grading:  Exact match against keyword-based ground truth
Baseline: 0.95 (excellent performance)
Method:   Keyword inference for deterministic ground truth

TASK 2: TIMETABLE SCHEDULING (Medium)
──────────────────────────────────────
Input:    List[ScheduleConstraint(class_name, teacher, duration, students, preferences)]
Output:   ScheduleAction(schedule: List[ScheduleSlot(day, start_time, end_time, location)])
Grading:  Multi-criteria evaluation:
          • No time clashes (50% weight)
          • Duration compliance (35% weight)
          • Preference satisfaction (15% weight)
Baseline: 0.87 (good performance)
Method:   Constraint satisfaction + optimization bonus

TASK 3: STUDENT SUPPORT RESPONSE (Hard)
────────────────────────────────────────
Input:    StudentQuery(student_name, issue_type, query, context)
Output:   SupportResponse(response, action_items, urgency)
Grading:  Multi-criteria evaluation:
          • Keyword coverage (25% weight)
          • Correctness (40% weight)
          • Tone appropriateness (25% weight)
          • Action items (10% weight)
Baseline: 0.82 (solid performance)
Method:   Multi-criteria rubric-based scoring
